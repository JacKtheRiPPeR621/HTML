First Section

Netflix released The Queen's Gambit on October 23, 2020. After four weeks it had become Netflix's most-watched scripted miniseries.[1] It was acclaimed by critics, who lauded Taylor-Joy's performance as well as the cinematography and production values. It has also received a positive response from the chess community and is claimed to have increased public interest in the game.[2][3] The series garnered two Golden Globe Awards nominations,[4] including Best Limited Series or Television Film. For her performance, Anya Taylor-Joy received nominations for the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Miniseries or Television Film, the Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Movie/Miniseries and the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Miniseries or Television Movie. The series garnered two Golden Globe Awards nominations,[4] including Best Limited Series or Television Film.
